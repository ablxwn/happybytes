Happiness Pie

Research has challenged the age-old belief that our genes are the sole architects of our happiness.
In 2005, the Happiness Pie experiment revealed that genes (in green) contribute only 50% to happiness,
leaving the other half to be shaped by external factors (in blue) and individual experiences (in red).

So, picture this: You, have the power to influence your own happiness.

Prosocial Spending:

The Gift of Happiness

In this global symphony of joy, the act of giving resonates as a melody that transcends borders. 

In a study involving participants from Canada (N=140), India (N=101), and Uganda (N=700), individuals reported higher levels of happiness when reflecting on spending money on others compared to spending on themselves.

The positive emotional impact of generous spending was observed across participants from both wealthy and economically challenged nations.

In a separate study with 207 students from Canada and South Africa, participants were given the opportunity to spend a small amount of money on an edible treat for themselves or to be donated to a sick child at a local hospital.

The results revealed that individuals who chose to purchase items for others experienced higher levels of happiness. This effect was consistent not only in Canada, where financial hardship was less prevalent, but also in South Africa, where more than 20% of respondents reported difficulties securing food for their families in the past year.

This exploration of the impact of prosocial spending on individual well-being opens
a captivating window into the interconnected realms of generosity and happiness,
which evidently transcends economic disparities and cultural contexts.
The Rollercoaster of Life:

Lottery Wins & Adversity

Life, like a rollercoaster, propels us through highs and lows.
Imagine a group that won the lottery (in green), riding the exhilarating ascent of joy,
and another group facing life-altering accidents (in red), descending into the abyss of despair.
Initially, the contrast is stark, but here's the twist: over time, both groups find themselves on similar plains of happiness.

This intriguing dance between fortune and adversity paints a vivid picture of human resilience.
It speaks to our innate ability to adapt, finding equilibrium in the face of life's unpredictable twists.
The pursuit of happiness, it seems, is not a linear journey but a dynamic dance with the unexpected.

A Global Tapestry

More specifically, World Happiness statistics on "Generosity" shed light on the interconnected nature of our joy.
Countries that prioritize generosity consistently find themselves atop happiness scales.
The chloropleth below shows the correlation between World Happiness and Generosity in 2023.

"Generosity" is a measure of how much individuals in a particular country or region are inclined to help others or contribute to social causes without expecting something in return. In short, it's an indicator for prosocial behavior!

"Explained by Generosity" indicates the contribution of generosity to the overall observed outcome - our happiness!

So, for all the variables mentioned, the higher the value,
the higher the happiness / generosity / contribution of generosity to happiness.
